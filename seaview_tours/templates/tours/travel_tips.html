Essential Travel Tips

Expert advice to make your adventure safe, enjoyable, and unforgettable

Travel Planning Tips

Prepare for your adventure with these essential planning tips

Visa & Documentation

  • Check visa requirements based on your nationality
  • Apply for Uganda visa at least 2-4 weeks before travel
  • Ensure passport has 6+ months validity
  • Make copies of important documents
  • Carry passport photos for on-arrival visas
  • Register with your embassy if staying long-term

Packing Essentials

  • Lightweight, breathable clothing
  • Comfortable walking/hiking shoes
  • Rain jacket (especially for rainy seasons)
  • Sun protection (hat, sunglasses, sunscreen)
  • Binoculars for wildlife viewing
  • Power adapter (UK Type G)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Camera with extra batteries

Health & Safety

  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate required
  • Consult doctor about malaria prevention
  • Pack basic first aid kit
  • Drink only bottled or purified water
  • Use insect repellent in evenings
  • Carry hand sanitizer
  • Get comprehensive travel insurance
  • Keep emergency contact numbers handy

Safari & Wildlife Tips

Make the most of your wildlife experiences in Uganda

Photography Tips

  • Golden hours (sunrise/sunset) for best light
  • Use zoom lens for wildlife photography
  • Pack extra memory cards and batteries
  • Respect animals - don't use flash
  • Capture local culture respectfully
  • Backup photos daily

Gorilla Trekking

  • Book permits 6+ months in advance
  • Good physical fitness required
  • Wear comfortable waterproof boots
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks
  • Follow guide's instructions strictly
  • Maintain 7m distance from gorillas

National Park Rules

  • Stay in vehicle during game drives
  • Don't feed or approach wildlife
  • Keep noise levels low
  • Stay on marked trails during hikes
  • No littering - pack out what you pack in
  • Respect park opening/closing times

Cultural Etiquette

Respect local customs and traditions during your visit

Greetings

Handshakes are common
Use right hand for giving/receiving
Greet elders first as sign of respect

Dress Code

Modest clothing appreciated
Cover shoulders and knees in rural areas
Respect religious site dress codes

Photography

Always ask permission before photographing people
Some communities may request small payment
Respect "no photography" signs

General Etiquette

Remove shoes when entering homes
Use titles when addressing professionals
Punctuality is flexible ("Uganda time")
Accept hospitality graciously

Budget & Money Tips

Smart financial planning for your Uganda trip

Money Management

  • Carry mix of cash and cards
  • Inform bank of travel plans
  • Use ATMs in secure locations
  • Keep small bills for tips and markets
  • Use money belt or hidden pouch
  • Track daily expenses

Shopping Tips

  • Bargain politely in markets
  • Support local artisans
  • Check wood/ivory item regulations
  • Keep receipts for customs
  • Quality varies - inspect carefully
  • Consider shipping large items home

Seasonal Savings

  • Low season (Apr-May, Oct-Nov) for discounts
  • Book flights 2-3 months in advance
  • Consider group tours for savings
  • Look for package deals
  • Travel mid-week for cheaper flights
  • Ask about last-minute deals
